Project Compound
October 2022

The primary objective of Project Compound was to design and construct a compound machine that could facilitate repetitive tasks in everyday life. Our team achieved this by combining four simple machines to create a device capable of holding an object weighing up to 250 grams without human intervention.
As the project manager, I was responsible for organizing and coordinating our team's efforts, as well as actively participating in the development of the physical prototype and online documentation. As someone with a background in computer science, I gained valuable insights into the added complexity of engineering projects. The necessity of physically constructing a prototype emphasized the importance of effective time management, and I learned the significance of establishing online communication channels within the team in order to maximize our building time.
One of the greatest challenges we faced was the management of time. Poor planning in the initial design and brainstorming stages led to a rushed process in the construction of our prototype.
As the project manager, I demonstrated strong leadership skills by effectively managing the team's time and coordinating our efforts. I also took an active role in the work, showing my willingness to get involved and contribute to the project. I demonstrated the ability to delegate tasks and delegate responsibility, as well as effectively communicate with my team, both in-person and online. I also demonstrated problem-solving skills by identifying and addressing the challenge of time management, and adapting to the added complexity of an engineering project despite my background in computer science.
