Work Experience

During my (short) time in high school, I have gained work experience through building collaborative programming projects with companies. Near the end of 8th grade, I travelled to Louisiana to intern at General Informatics. At General Informatics, I built several Windows servers and was responsible for setting up RAID (data storage virtualization). I did not know exactly how to set up RAID before this experience, so I learned how to do so. At the same time, however, I also learned about the importance of asking questions in a professional environment. There was no shame in asking even the most basic of questions there - I was told that it is better that you make the initial investment of asking questions so you can accomplish your assigned tasks without any hiccups.

I also interned at 365Labs where I designed and programmed parts of a web interface for the public to interact with police departments. In this experience, I learned about the importance of clear communication. Most of the team I was a part of using Slack or email, so it was inevitable that I would have learn how to communicate clearly.

Career Research

In Freshman Seminar, I researched several careers that would match my interests. Two of the most prominent careers that would fit me were business intelligence analysts and medical health service managers. Business intelligence analysts use data science to identify market trends, which fit in with my desire to be a programmer but also work in a position closely tied to business. Medical health service managers are less technical and focus on administrating a hospital in both its medical and administrative components.
From this experience, I learned about how multiple fields can be closely tied together (programming & business or administration & health). This led me to explore more careers that I desire to work in or degrees that I want to work for, as I was now aware of how two fields can be closely connected.
