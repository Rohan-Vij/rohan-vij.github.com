So, who am I?

I sincerely believe that leaders of any type should not only command a group of people, they should work with them and inspire them to become great leaders themselves. In order to become a great leader, I desire to attend a four-year college and receive a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, and then earn my Master of Business Administration degree after some amount of work experience. Leading multiple groups, whether they be group projects or entire classes, made me discover my passion for management and leadership. As a result, I aspire to work in a leadership position where I can change the lives of my team in and out of the workplace.

I am currently the president and founder of ACE Coding Dublin, a programming organizations where other teachers and I work to educate other high school and middle school students about technology and programming concepts. Although this is not my first time educating other students, this opportunity has made me learn a lot more about leadership and management. I learned the virtue of planning and not procrastinating - when we were a school club, we obtained an advisor early and avoided the chaos of last-minute emails. I also learned the importance of direct communication, as when you have to communicate with a group of almost fifty people you need to make your message short and to the point to maintain their attention. I also lead my school's Environmental Technology club, which built our school's greenspace and is working on developing an ROV!

My experience coding and being in a family of entrepreneurs and software leaders has also greatly impacted my perspective of life. I interned at a company, 365Labs, which manufactures software and hardware primarily for police use. Working with the company and contributing to some projects makes my heart swell with pride, even though I was, of course, not a key member. Just knowing that something I may have contributed to may have saved lives makes all the difference. I look forward to more opportunities with other companies to benefit society and save lives. My passion for programming is strongly connected to projects I made which have the capability to help real people, which is why I desire to dedicate my life to programming and people.
